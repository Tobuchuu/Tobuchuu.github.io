Religion

Taoism, Buddhism, Islam, and Catholicism are all religions that have integrated into chinese culture throughout history. These religions have also been recognized by the government. Along with national pride, religion is a distinguishing characteristic for many Chinese people.

  • Buddhism

    About 2,000 years ago, Buddhism spread from India to China. The majority of Buddhist followers are Han Chinese, but Tibetan Buddhist are also a sizable proportion. It’s the most dominant religion who is the most dominant throughout chinese communities.


  • Taoism

    Taoism is a Chinese religion with a history dating back more than 1,700 years. Lao Tzu founded it, and the teachings are based on his writings about the Tao or Way. You may even be familiar with some Taoism symbols. The well-known Yin and Yang symbol is a fundamental depiction of Taoist beliefs. It represents the balance of dark and light.


  • Islam

    Islam origens from the middle east and came to China more than 1,300 years ago. Today, it currently has almost 14 million followers. The followers mostly live in the northwest parts of China like Xinjiang, Ningxia, Ganxu and Qinghai.


  • Christianity

    Christianity made its way to China very early and rapidly developed in 1840 after the Sino-British Opium War. The number and influence of Chinese Catholic and Christian communities rose across the country. The religion took some time to get a solid footing in China, but it is now firmly entrenched.
